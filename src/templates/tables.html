NMR Spectroscopy

Nuclear Magnetic Resonance (NMR) spectroscopy is a technique used to determine the chemical structure and purity of compounds. It is based on the principle that certain atomic nuclei have magnetic properties.

Key features of NMR spectroscopy:

  • Determines molecular structure by analyzing the magnetic properties of atomic nuclei.
  • Useful in identifying the carbon-hydrogen framework of organic compounds.
  • Commonly used nuclei: 1H, 13C.

IR Spectroscopy

Infrared (IR) spectroscopy is a technique used to analyze the chemical bonds present in a compound. It measures the absorption of infrared radiation by the sample, which is related to the vibrational modes of the chemical bonds.

Key features of IR spectroscopy:

  • Identifies functional groups based on absorption of IR radiation.
  • Useful in determining molecular structure and identifying compounds.
  • Provides a fingerprint region unique to each compound.

Example IR Spectral Table

2850-2950 Strong C-H Stretch

Mass Spectrometry

Mass Spectrometry is a technique used to identify the mass and structure of chemical compounds. It ionizes chemical species and sorts the ions based on their mass-to-charge ratio.

Key features of Mass Spectrometry:

  • Determines molecular weight and structure of compounds.
  • Provides information about the molecular formula.
  • Useful in identifying unknown compounds and analyzing complex mixtures.
